Pillar 01 — World models

LLMs predict text,
not consequences.

Scaling token prediction does not build an internal model of the world. Feed agents principles & metamodels that let them compose world-context and reason about actions.

Pillar 02 — Beyond the perceptron

All of AI descends
from one neuron.

Deep learning traces to a single template — the mammalian cortical neuron. Intelligence takes many forms; the perceptron lineage has explored a sliver of the design space.

01The wager

Two gaps in modern AI. One opportunity between them.

The agentic era is a chance to combine today's LLMs with architectural primitives borrowed from organisms that compute nothing like us — fungi, octopuses, birds, bacteria, viruses.

Pillar 01 · Guiding principle

The world-model gap

Yann LeCun's critique: a system that only predicts the next token cannot plan, cannot model cause and effect, cannot ground language in a world. The fix isn't more scale — it's structure that represents consequences.

Guiding principle for what we give the machine.
Pillar 02 · The search space

The biological fan

Intelligence evolved independently across phyla — acerebrate optimization in slime molds, distributed cognition in bird flocks, quorum decisions in bacteria, embodiment without cortex in octopuses. Each is an unborrowed design pattern.

The opportunity space we search for the machine.

03Worked example · end to end

A zebra finch eavesdrops. An AI agent gets a world model.

One thread, all four assets — how a bird watching a fight becomes a drop-in pattern for multi-agent systems.

Source · peer-reviewedBiology

Wild zebra finches extract information from fights they only watch

Communication Networks of Wild Taeniopygia castanotis — bioRxiv, 2026. Finches that observe third-party song interactions learn who is dominant, who is bonded, who is a threat — without ever participating.

Repo · engineeringNEXI pattern

Turned into a falsifiable, engineerable pattern

The hub graduates into the catalog as eavesdropping — a machine-readable pattern with a testable claim and drop-in formats (architecture + skill). 

# nexi/eavesdropping/nexi.yaml
abstract_claim: Agents that observe but do not participate can still
  extract goals, knowledge, capabilities & relationships.
falsifiable_hypothesis: Multi-agent RL with attention over
  observed third-party interactions reaches threshold in
  ≥10% fewer episodes than self-observation-only baselines.
applies_to: [multi-agent, world-models, llm-orchestration, dec-POMDP]
formats: [architecture, skill]
provenance: {hub: Eavesdropping, principle: P02, metamodel: MM1}
Vault · demandAI Research gap

Aimed at a documented capability gap

The pattern targets a real limitation in the SOTA catalog: agents in partially-observable, multi-agent settings that cannot build peer models from indirect observation. The gap's nature_evidence field now has a candidate.

06The business lens
Why a program like this creates value

A defensible catalog of AI design patterns, mined from 3.8 billion years of R&D.

01 · The bet

An untapped design space

Everyone is scaling the same architecture. The frontier advantage goes to whoever borrows primitives no one else has — biology is a vast, unexplored library of proven intelligence.

02 · The moat

Provenance & falsifiability

Not inspiration decks — a curated, cited, testable pattern catalog with a documented method. That rigor is the difference between defensible IP and a metaphor.

03 · Where value lands

Better agents, real credibility

Sample-efficient, world-grounded agents for partial-observability problems; a reusable pattern library for teams; and peer-reviewed publications that compound trust.
