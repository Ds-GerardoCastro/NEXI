# References — Multi-Modal Integration

## Primary natural-system literature (multi-source)

This is a **multi-source** NEXI: one behavioural source and one cross-phyletic anatomical source converge on the same integration commitment.

- **Hagedoorn, K., Tschirren, N., ter Avest, E., Tyson, C., Snijders, L., Griffith, S. C., Naguib, M., & Loning, H. (2026).** _Communication Networks of Wild Zebra Finches (Taeniopygia castanotis)._ bioRxiv preprint, posted 2026-04-27. DOI: [10.1101/2025.09.11.675577](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.09.11.675577). License CC-BY-NC-ND 4.0.
  _Empirical demonstration that song-based and spatial co-occurrence networks mirror each other in zebra finch colonies — vocal and spatial channels carry convergent information about the same underlying social state. The behavioural evidence for joint (not late-fusion) integration._

- **Roth, G. (2015).** _Convergent evolution of complex brains and high intelligence._ Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B, 370, 20150049. DOI: [10.1098/rstb.2015.0049](https://doi.org/10.1098/rstb.2015.0049).
  _Comparative synthesis documenting the convergent emergence of dedicated multimodal integration centres across five independent lineages — insect mushroom bodies, octopod vertical lobe, cichlid pallium, corvid/psittacid avian pallium, and mammalian isocortex. None are anatomical homologues, yet all share the commitment of integrating multiple sensory streams on a shared associative substrate. Quantitative circuit data (honeybee mushroom body ~300,000 Kenyon cells reading ~800 projection neurons; octopus vertical lobe ~26M interneurons converging on ~65,000 projection neurons) supports the joint-encoder reading at the substrate level. Second, independent, cross-phyletic and anatomical source — basis for canonical promotion (2026-07-04)._

The convergence of a wild-ethology source and a comparative-neuroanatomy source on the same architectural commitment, from opposite methodological poles, is load-bearing for this NEXI's elevation to canonical, multi-source status.

## Comparative-cognition context

- **Snijders, L., & Naguib, M. (2017).** _Communication in animal social networks: a missing link?_ Advances in the Study of Behavior, 49, 297–359.
  _Theoretical framework linking communication and movement networks; basis for treating cross-modal observation as fundamental rather than incidental._

## Computational analogs

- **Radford, A., et al. (2021).** _Learning Transferable Visual Models from Natural Language Supervision (CLIP)._ ICML 2021.
  _Foundational cross-modal contrastive alignment of image + text into a shared latent space._

- **Jia, C., et al. (2021).** _Scaling Up Visual and Vision-Language Representation Learning with Noisy Text Supervision (ALIGN)._ ICML 2021.
  _Same architectural choice as CLIP at scale; demonstrates the joint-encoding approach generalises._

- **Girdhar, R., et al. (2023).** _ImageBind: One Embedding Space To Bind Them All._ CVPR 2023.
  _Extension to six modalities (image, text, audio, depth, thermal, IMU) via shared latent space._

- **Assran, M., et al. (2023).** _Self-Supervised Learning from Images with a Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (I-JEPA)._ CVPR 2023.
  _Predict in a joint latent space rather than per-modality; theoretically aligned with the spirit of this NEXI._

- **Bardes, A., et al. (2024).** _V-JEPA: Latent Video Prediction for Visual Representation Learning._ arXiv preprint.
  _Video-domain extension of JEPA; central to the broader Nature Intelligence Project's architectural argument._
