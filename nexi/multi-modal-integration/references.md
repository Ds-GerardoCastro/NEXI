# References — Multi-Modal Integration

## Primary natural-system literature

### Behavioural-network scale (zebra finch)

- **Hagedoorn, K., Tschirren, N., ter Avest, E., Tyson, C., Snijders, L., Griffith, S. C., Naguib, M., & Loning, H. (2026).** _Communication Networks of Wild Zebra Finches (Taeniopygia castanotis)._ bioRxiv preprint, posted 2026-04-27. DOI: [10.1101/2025.09.11.675577](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.09.11.675577). License CC-BY-NC-ND 4.0.
  _Empirical demonstration that song-based and spatial co-occurrence networks mirror each other in zebra finch colonies — vocal and spatial channels carry convergent information at the population/network level._

- **Snijders, L., & Naguib, M. (2017).** _Communication in animal social networks: a missing link?_ Advances in the Study of Behavior, **49**, 297–359.
  _Theoretical framework linking communication and movement networks; basis for treating cross-modal observation as fundamental rather than incidental._

### Molecular-substrate scale (bacteria)

- **Nesin, S. M., & Chandrankunnel, M. (2025).** _The need for a new perspective on decision-making in bacteria._ Communicative & Integrative Biology, **18**(1), 2463926. DOI: [10.1080/19420889.2025.2463926](https://doi.org/10.1080/19420889.2025.2463926). License CC BY 4.0. Peer-reviewed review article.
  _Documents the ~10,000-chemoreceptor sensor array per E. coli cell with multiple binding sites each (BphP / Tsr / Tar / Trg / FixL), integrating light, pH, temperature, sugars, and oxygen into a single regulatory state. Multi-modal integration is observable at the molecular-sensor level — same architectural commitment as the zebra finch network exemplar, on a substrate with no nervous system at all._

### Circuit-level scale (cross-phyletic synthesis)

- **Roth, G. (2015).** _Convergent evolution of complex brains and high intelligence._ Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B: Biological Sciences, **370**(1684), 20150049. DOI: [10.1098/rstb.2015.0049](https://doi.org/10.1098/rstb.2015.0049). Peer-reviewed review article in a themed issue on the origins of intelligence.
  _Reviews convergent emergence of multi-modal integration centres across five independent lineages: insect mushroom bodies (Hymenoptera, Lepidoptera, Odonata, Blattoidea), octopod vertical lobe (Cephalopoda), cichlid pallium (Teleostei), corvid/psittacid avian pallium (Aves), and mammalian isocortex. Quantitative circuit cytoarchitecture — honeybee mushroom body (300 000 Kenyon cells × 800 projection neurons via ~1M presynaptic contacts) and octopus vertical lobe (26M tiny interneurons × 65 000 projection neurons + 1.8M afferent fibres in an orthogonal en-passant crossbar) — provides the substrate-level evidence behind the joint-encoder reading. Roth's cross-phyletic synthesis lifts this NEXI to canonical status by adding circuit-level evidence on five non-homologous neural substrates._

## Computational analogs

- **Radford, A., et al. (2021).** _Learning Transferable Visual Models from Natural Language Supervision (CLIP)._ ICML 2021.
  _Foundational cross-modal contrastive alignment of image + text into a shared latent space._

- **Jia, C., et al. (2021).** _Scaling Up Visual and Vision-Language Representation Learning with Noisy Text Supervision (ALIGN)._ ICML 2021.
  _Same architectural choice as CLIP at scale; demonstrates the joint-encoding approach generalises._

- **Girdhar, R., et al. (2023).** _ImageBind: One Embedding Space To Bind Them All._ CVPR 2023.
  _Extension to six modalities (image, text, audio, depth, thermal, IMU) via shared latent space._

- **Assran, M., et al. (2023).** _Self-Supervised Learning from Images with a Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (I-JEPA)._ CVPR 2023.
  _Predict in a joint latent space rather than per-modality; theoretically aligned with the spirit of this NEXI._

- **Bardes, A., et al. (2024).** _V-JEPA: Latent Video Prediction for Visual Representation Learning._ arXiv preprint.
  _Video-domain extension of JEPA; central to the broader Nature Intelligence Project's architectural argument._

## Supporting comparative-neuroanatomy literature

- **Strausfeld, N. J. (2012).** _Arthropod Brains: Evolution, Functional Elegance, and Historical Significance._ Harvard University Press.
  _Foundational reference for insect mushroom-body cytoarchitecture; cited by Roth 2015 for the Hymenoptera Kenyon-cell numbers._

- **Hochner, B. (2012).** _An embodied view of octopus neurobiology._ Current Biology, **22**(20), R887–R892.
  _Octopus vertical-lobe architecture and its role as the cognitive locus; cited by Roth 2015 for the cephalopod cytoarchitecture._

- **Shomrat, T., Zarrella, I., Fiorito, G., & Hochner, B. (2008).** _The octopus vertical lobe modulates short-term learning rate and uses LTP to acquire long-term memory._ Current Biology, **18**(5), 337–342. DOI: [10.1016/j.cub.2008.01.056](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cub.2008.01.056).
  _Primary electrophysiological evidence for octopus vertical-lobe learning role; lesion-defined cognitive bottleneck (Roth 2015)._
