# References — Network Density Over Absolute Size

## Primary natural-system literature

- **Roth, G. (2015).** _Convergent evolution of complex brains and high intelligence._ Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B, **370**(1684), 20150049. DOI: [10.1098/rstb.2015.0049](https://doi.org/10.1098/rstb.2015.0049).
  _Comparative synthesis establishing that the correlates of high intelligence are network-quality metrics — neuron number, packing density, connection number and pattern, cell-type diversity — rather than absolute brain size. Supplies both anchoring facts for this NEXI: large-brained elephants and cetaceans rank below denser corvids and primates, and honeybee Kenyon-cell packing density runs ~15× the highest density found in the vertebrate brain. Primary and sole source for the NEXI's quantitative claims._

## Supporting / further reading

- **Moroz, L. L. (2009).** _On the independent origins of complex brains and neurons._ Brain, Behavior and Evolution, **74**(3), 177–190. DOI: [10.1159/000258665](https://doi.org/10.1159/000258665).
  _Argues for multiple independent origins of complex nervous systems and neurons. Provides the wider comparative-neurobiology backdrop for treating substrate-agnostic network metrics (rather than a single conserved plan or absolute size) as the right unit of comparison across independently evolved brains._
