# References — Heuristics as Habits Fusion

## Primary natural-system literature

- **Nordli, S. A., & Todd, P. M. (2022).** _Embodied and embedded ecological rationality: A common vertebrate mechanism for action selection underlies cognition and heuristic decision-making in humans._ Frontiers in Psychology, **13**, 841972. DOI: [10.3389/fpsyg.2022.841972](https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2022.841972). License CC BY. Peer-reviewed conceptual-analysis article.
  _Source paper develops the formal equivalence between heuristic decision-making and motor-habit-formation in §6 and §7. The recognition-heuristic worked example (Tokyo / Yokohama / Nagasaki, drawing on Goldstein & Gigerenzer 2002) demonstrates that heuristic-based errors are formally indistinguishable from habit-based errors via the Ecological Context Model's equation (3)._

## Foundational ecological-rationality / adaptive-toolbox literature

- **Goldstein, D. G., & Gigerenzer, G. (2002).** _Models of ecological rationality: The recognition heuristic._ Psychological Review, **109**(1), 75–90. DOI: [10.1037/0033-295X.109.1.75](https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-295X.109.1.75).
  *Foundational paper on the recognition heuristic. Establishes the *less-is-more* effect and the conditions under which the heuristic succeeds vs. fails. Cited extensively by Nordli & Todd 2022 as the empirical anchor for the recognition-heuristic worked example.*

- **Gigerenzer, G., & Todd, P. M. (1999).** _Simple heuristics that make us smart._ Oxford University Press.
  _Foundational volume of the adaptive-toolbox programme. The toolbox is a library of niche-adapted heuristics; this NEXI's unified-repertoire architecture is the engineering operationalisation. Cited by Nordli & Todd 2022. Cited by canonical bibliographic record per the catalog's no-DOI quarantine rule for this monograph._

- **Tversky, A. (1972).** _Elimination by aspects: A theory of choice._ Psychological Review, **79**(4), 281–299. DOI: [10.1037/h0032955](https://doi.org/10.1037/h0032955).
  _Foundational paper on the elimination-by-aspects heuristic. Cited by Nordli & Todd 2022 as a worked example in §6._

- **Todd, P. M., Gigerenzer, G., & the ABC Research Group. (2012).** _Ecological rationality: Intelligence in the world._ Oxford University Press. DOI: [10.1093/acprof:oso/9780195315448.001.0001](https://doi.org/10.1093/acprof:oso/9780195315448.001.0001).
  _Comprehensive ecological-rationality treatment. The unified-repertoire architecture in this NEXI is structurally close to the adaptive-toolbox framing but adds explicit cost-aware competition and cross-domain error analysis._

## Dual-process / two-systems literature (the alternative this NEXI replaces)

- **Kahneman, D. (2011).** _Thinking, fast and slow._ Farrar, Straus and Giroux.
  _Popular synthesis of the dual-process / System 1 / System 2 framework. The default mental model the heuristics-as-habits-fusion NEXI argues against at the architectural level. Cited by canonical bibliographic record per the catalog's no-DOI quarantine rule for this trade-press monograph._

- **Stanovich, K. E., & West, R. F. (2000).** _Individual differences in reasoning: Implications for the rationality debate?_ Behavioral and Brain Sciences, **23**(5), 645–665. DOI: [10.1017/S0140525X00003435](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0140525X00003435).
  _Influential paper formalising the System 1 / System 2 distinction. The dual-process architecture this NEXI proposes to unify is grounded in this literature._

- **Evans, J. St. B. T., & Stanovich, K. E. (2013).** _Dual-process theories of higher cognition: Advancing the debate._ Perspectives on Psychological Science, **8**(3), 223–241. DOI: [10.1177/1745691612460685](https://doi.org/10.1177/1745691612460685).
  _Recent review of dual-process theories. Useful as a counter-position to this NEXI's unified-mechanism claim._

## Computational analogs

### Cognitive architectures with utility-based competition

- **Anderson, J. R., Bothell, D., Byrne, M. D., Douglass, S., Lebiere, C., & Qin, Y. (2004).** _An integrated theory of the mind._ Psychological Review, **111**(4), 1036–1060. DOI: [10.1037/0033-295X.111.4.1036](https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-295X.111.4.1036).
  _ACT-R cognitive architecture — production-system with utility-based conflict resolution. Closest existing analog of this NEXI's cost-aware competition mechanism. Heuristic-style and deliberative-style productions compete in the same conflict-resolution step._

### Bounded-rationality AI

- **Russell, S. J., & Wefald, E. (1991).** _Do the right thing: Studies in limited rationality._ MIT Press.
  _Foundational treatment of bounded rationality in AI. Cost-aware competition is structurally related to the limited-rationality-as-meta-decision framing. Cited by canonical bibliographic record per the catalog's no-DOI quarantine rule for this monograph._

- **Lieder, F., & Griffiths, T. L. (2020).** _Resource-rational analysis: Understanding human cognition as the optimal use of limited computational resources._ Behavioral and Brain Sciences, **43**, e1. DOI: [10.1017/S0140525X1900061X](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0140525X1900061X).
  _Recent resource-rational framework — modelling cognition as cost-aware competition over heuristics and deliberation. Closest contemporary AI analog of this NEXI's claim that cost-awareness can replace dual-process routing._

### Mixture-of-strategies in reinforcement learning

- **Daw, N. D., Gershman, S. J., Seymour, B., Dayan, P., & Dolan, R. J. (2011).** _Model-based influences on humans' choices and striatal prediction errors._ Neuron, **69**(6), 1204–1215. DOI: [10.1016/j.neuron.2011.02.027](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2011.02.027).
  _Empirical paper on the model-based / model-free distinction in human RL. Often invoked as a neural correlate of System 1 / System 2; this NEXI's unification claim implies the distinction is descriptive rather than architectural._
