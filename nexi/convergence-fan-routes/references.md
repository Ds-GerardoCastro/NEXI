# References — Substrate-Diverse Routes to One Function

## Primary natural-system literature

- **Roth, G. (2015).** _Convergent evolution of complex brains and high intelligence._ Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B, **370**(1684), 20150049. DOI: [10.1098/rstb.2015.0049](https://doi.org/10.1098/rstb.2015.0049).
  _Comparative synthesis establishing that complex brains and high intelligence evolved independently in insects, cephalopods, teleosts, birds and mammals — taxa whose common ancestors had neither complex brains nor intelligent behaviour. The independence across non-homologous, developmentally divergent substrates is the primary evidence for the convergence-fan shape: many routes, one functional attractor._

## Supporting literature — falsifier of the single-driver account

- **Farris, S. M., & Schulmeister, S. (2011).** _Parasitoidism, not sociality, is associated with the evolution of elaborate mushroom bodies in the brains of hymenopteran insects._ Proceedings of the Royal Society B, **278**(1707), 940–951. DOI: [10.1098/rspb.2010.2161](https://doi.org/10.1098/rspb.2010.2161).
  _Shows that among hymenopteran insects, elaborate mushroom bodies track parasitoidism rather than sociality — falsifying the tidy single-driver ("social brain") account for this lineage. Directly relevant to the fan claim: the same destination (an elaborate associative centre) is reached under different selective pressures, so route multiplicity extends to the driving forces, not just the anatomy. Cited here as the guard against collapsing "many routes" into "one driver."_
