# References — Convergent Integration Hub

## Primary natural-system literature

- **Roth, G. (2015).** _Convergent evolution of complex brains and high intelligence._ Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B, **370**(1684), 20150049. DOI: [10.1098/rstb.2015.0049](https://doi.org/10.1098/rstb.2015.0049).
  _Comparative synthesis documenting that high intelligence is invariantly bound to a discrete multimodal associative centre — mushroom bodies (insects), vertical lobe (octopodids), pallium (birds), cerebral cortex (primates) — each a dense associative network, none homologous to the others. Establishes the integration-hub as a functional-role invariant realised on non-homologous tissue. Primary source for this NEXI's invariant claim._

## Supporting mechanism literature

- **Shomrat, T., Zarrella, I., Fiorito, G., & Hochner, B. (2008).** _The octopus vertical lobe modulates short-term learning rate and uses LTP to store learned information._ Current Biology, **18**(5), 337–342. DOI: [10.1016/j.cub.2008.01.056](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cub.2008.01.056).
  _Isolates the cephalopod vertical lobe as the associative-integration organ: it modulates short-term learning rate. Directly relevant here as the mechanistic evidence that the "vertical lobe" entry in Roth's list of convergent hubs is functionally an integration/association centre, not merely an anatomical landmark._

- **Jarvis, E. D., Güntürkün, O., Bruce, L., Csillag, A., Karten, H., Kuenzel, W., et al. (2005).** _Avian brains and a new understanding of vertebrate brain evolution._ Nature Reviews Neuroscience, **6**(2), 151–159. DOI: [10.1038/nrn1606](https://doi.org/10.1038/nrn1606).
  _The revised account of avian pallial organisation that reframed the bird forebrain as a large associative telencephalon rather than a primitive striatum. Supports the "pallium/nidopallium as multimodal associative hub" entry in the convergent-hub list._
