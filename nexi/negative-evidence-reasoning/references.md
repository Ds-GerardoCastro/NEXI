# References — Negative-Evidence Reasoning

## Primary natural-system literature

- **Hagedoorn, K., Tschirren, N., ter Avest, E., Tyson, C., Snijders, L., Griffith, S. C., Naguib, M., & Loning, H. (2025).** _Communication Networks of Wild Zebra Finches (Taeniopygia castanotis)._ bioRxiv preprint, posted 2025-09-11. DOI: [10.1101/2025.09.11.675577](https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.09.11.675577). License CC-BY-NC-ND 4.0. Unrefereed preprint; observational.
  _Reports no dawn-song peak in wild zebra finches and reads that missing peak as consistent with a non-territorial social system — an absence used as diagnostic evidence. Natural analog only; not empirical support for the ML hypothesis._

- **Snijders, L., & Naguib, M. (2017).** _Communication in animal social networks: a missing link?_ Advances in the Study of Behavior, 49, 297–359.
  _Synthesis on signal function in animal social networks, including how the presence and structure of signalling (and its absence) map onto social systems._

## Documented AI need — negation insensitivity

- **Ettinger, A. (2020).** _What BERT Is Not: Lessons from a New Suite of Psycholinguistic Diagnostics for Language Models._ Transactions of the Association for Computational Linguistics (TACL), 8, 34–48. arXiv:1907.13528.
  _Diagnostic evidence that BERT is largely insensitive to negation — it produces nearly the same expectations for an affirmative statement and its negation, failing to let "not" reverse its predictions. Primary evidence that reasoning-from-absence / negation is an unmet need._

- **Kassner, N., & Schütze, H. (2020).** _Negated and Misprimed Probes for Pretrained Language Models: Birds Can Talk, But Cannot Fly._ Proceedings of ACL 2020, 7811–7818. arXiv:1911.03343.
  _Shows pretrained language models often assign higher probability to the false completion of a negated statement than to the true one, on negated-LAMA probes. Supplies the named, pre-registerable benchmark for this NEXI's falsifiable hypothesis._

## Mechanistic substrate

- **Friston, K. (2010).** _The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory?_ Nature Reviews Neuroscience, 11(2), 127–138.
  _Predictive-coding / prediction-error framework — a system maintains explicit expectations and treats deviations (including expected-but-absent observations) as the signal. The substrate for noticing what is not there._

- **Pearl, J. (2009).** _Causality: Models, Reasoning, and Inference._ Cambridge University Press, 2nd ed.
  _Foundational treatment of counterfactual reasoning — interpreting an absence requires asking what would have been observed under alternative hypotheses and comparing to what was._
