## 9.1 Recap

Planned coverage: the two-pillar critique (architectural + template), their convergence on a single research direction, the methodology that operationalises it, and the catalog (NEXI) that delivers engineering-ready design primitives.

## 9.2 Contribution claim

Planned coverage: what is genuinely new in this work.

- A _named, structured critique_ of AI's narrow biological inspiration (the template pillar), articulated alongside the more familiar architectural critique.
- A _methodology_ for translating peer-reviewed comparative cognition into AI design primitives, with provenance and falsifiability preserved end-to-end.
- An _open catalog_ (NEXI) that puts the methodology into practice, organised at two layers (clusters and patterns) with disciplined curation.
- A _demonstration case_ (Distributed Social Cognition) showing the methodology produces engineering-ready primitives with peer-reviewable provenance.

## 9.3 Position in the conversation

Planned coverage: the relationship of the work to mainstream AI research (complement, not displacement), to comparative-cognition literature (purposive mining, not survey), and to the broader debate about scaling vs. structure. The white paper is the foundation; the catalog is the long arc; the program is open and ongoing.

## 9.4 Closing note

Intelligence as it actually exists in biological species takes many forms. Modern AI has explored a small slice of that design space. This work is an invitation — to AI engineers, to biologists, to philosophers of mind — to widen the slice deliberately, with the discipline that lets the resulting design library remain defensible, falsifiable, and useful.
