# 9. Conclusion

## 9.1 Recap

The argument of this white paper rests on two converging critiques of modern AI: the architectural critique that LLM-based agentic systems lack the world models required for reliable planning, and the template critique that AI's biological inspiration descends from a single perceptron lineage and has barely engaged with the broader intelligence-design space documented across species. The two critiques converge on a single research direction: world models should be informed by the plurality of natural intelligence designs, not by scaling up one cortical template.

The methodology of Section 4 operationalises that direction: a pipeline that turns peer-reviewed comparative-cognition literature into engineering-ready architectural primitives, with provenance preserved, falsifiability required, and citation discipline enforced end-to-end. The framework of Section 5 organises the resulting primitives at two layers — patterns and clusters — and recognises five distinct cluster shapes that have emerged from the catalog so far: constellation, pipeline, composition (the three shapes that emerged from the first three clusters), isomorphism (the fourth shape, contributed by a subsequently-ingested vertebrate-action-selection source), and convergence-fan (the fifth, contributed by a comparative-neuroanatomy source on the independent evolution of intelligence [Roth 2015]).

The case studies of Section 6 walk through the first three clusters as worked examples; §6.6 and §6.7 summarise the fourth and fifth clusters as addenda. The catalog at version 1 contains eighteen patterns across five clusters drawn from five peer-reviewed sources, with two patterns (`niche-specification` and `action-selection-as-common-substrate`) promoted to canonical — the first on convergent two-paper evidence, the second on cross-substrate triangulation across vertebrate-CBGTC, bacterial-molecular, and cnidarian-nerve-net implementations. The shape diversity is itself an empirical finding — nature does not assemble adaptive behaviour in one canonical way, and a catalog that recognises this can place architectural claims at the shape level, not only at the pattern level.

## 9.2 Contribution claim

Four contributions are argued for in this work.

A _named, structured critique_ of AI's narrow biological inspiration — the template pillar — articulated alongside the more familiar architectural critique. The two-pillar framing is itself the contribution; neither pillar alone suffices to motivate the research direction the program targets.

A _methodology_ for translating peer-reviewed comparative cognition into AI design primitives, with provenance and falsifiability preserved end-to-end and a deterministic citation-verification gate enforced before any external publication.

An _open catalog_ (NEXI) that puts the methodology into practice — eighteen patterns across five clusters, organised at two layers, with disciplined curation and an explicit canonical-promotion criterion that has now been satisfied by two patterns (one via convergent-two-paper evidence, one via cross-substrate triangulation).

A _shape-diversity finding_ at the cluster level: constellation, pipeline, composition, isomorphism, and convergence-fan are five architecturally distinct ways the catalog has already documented adaptive behaviour assembling itself. The framework is open to additional shapes, and the empirical claim that shape diversity is real — strengthened by the fourth and fifth shapes' clean absorption into the framework after the three-shape finding was articulated, the fifth being the structural inverse of the fourth — is itself a research-relevant finding rather than an organisational convenience.

## 9.3 Position in the conversation

The work complements rather than displaces the world-model research direction; it challenges scaling-only framing, AGI-as-niche-abstracted-target, and pattern-bolt-on practices that ignore complementarity. It mines comparative cognition rather than imitating it. It inherits the pattern-catalog form from Alexander and the Gang-of-Four but commits to peer-reviewable provenance and per-pattern falsifiability that those traditions did not require. The white paper is the foundation; the catalog is the long arc; the program is open and ongoing.

## 9.4 Closing note

Intelligence as it actually exists in biological species takes many forms. Modern AI has explored a small slice of that design space. This work is an invitation — to AI engineers, to biologists, to philosophers of mind — to widen the slice deliberately, with the discipline that lets the resulting design library remain defensible, falsifiable, and useful.
