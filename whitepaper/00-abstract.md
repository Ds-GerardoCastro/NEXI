# Abstract

Modern agentic AI systems built on Large Language Models exhibit a structural failure mode: they cannot reliably plan multi-step behaviour because they lack an internal model of how their actions change the environment. This paper argues that the architectural critique — most clearly articulated by Yann LeCun — has a deeper companion that has not been fully named: AI's biological inspiration is narrow. Modern deep learning descends from a single template, the perceptron, modelled on a simplified mammalian cortical neuron, and propagated unchanged through eighty years of architectural variation. Intelligence as it actually exists in biological species takes many forms; AI has explored only a small slice of that design space.

This paper proposes the **Nature Intelligence Project** as a research program that addresses both critiques jointly. The architectural critique tells us _what is missing_ — internal world models grounded in sensory, not linguistic, prediction. The template critique tells us _where to find candidate designs_ — peer-reviewed comparative cognition across species. The two converge on a single research direction: world models should be informed by the _plurality_ of natural intelligence designs, not by scaling up one cortical template.

The paper introduces **NEXI (Nature Expression of Intelligence)**, the curated catalog that operationalises this direction. NEXI is organised at two layers: individual _patterns_ (architectural primitives and the unit of adoption) and _collections_ (groupings of patterns read from the same biological system, each advancing a falsifiable hypothesis that its patterns reinforce one another). Collections have so far taken three structural shapes — _constellation_ (mutually reinforcing patterns anchored to a shared niche), _pipeline_ (sequential dependency through capacity-limited stages), and _composition_ (substrate-independent components combined into adaptive behaviour) — and the diversity of these shapes is itself an empirical finding of the catalog: intelligence in nature does not assemble in one canonical way. Each entry is grounded in peer-reviewed natural-system literature, presented in a layered format addressing both engineering and academic audiences, and commits to a falsifiable architectural hypothesis even when no empirical test has yet been run.

Three demonstration case studies — one per collection shape — show how the methodology produces engineering-ready design primitives with peer-reviewable provenance: a _constellation_ collection (**Distributed Social Cognition**) derived from research on wild zebra finch communication networks; a _pipeline_ collection (**Bounded Cognitive Architecture**) derived from work on cognitive-capacity limits in non-human animals; and a _composition_ collection (**Acerebrate Decision-Making**) derived from research on bacterial decision-making without nervous systems. A fourth source — Roth's cross-phyletic synthesis of the independent evolution of complex brains and high intelligence [Roth 2015] — was ingested subsequently: it promotes **Multi-Modal Integration** to the catalog's second canonical pattern (a dedicated multimodal integration centre recurs convergently across five non-homologous lineages, on top of the zebra-finch behavioural evidence), contributes a new circuit-motif pattern (`expansion-readout-circuit`, the expand-then-read-out motif), and seeds a fourth, deliberately young collection (**Substrate-Independent Cognition**, single-source) grouping the architectural commitments that recur across substrates. Together these anchor a present catalog of thirteen patterns across four collections drawn from four peer-reviewed sources, with two patterns (`niche-specification` and `multi-modal-integration`) promoted to canonical status on multi-source evidence. The three mature collections yield the three structural shapes above; the fourth collection is newly seeded and not yet assigned a distinct shape. The catalog and methodology are presented as the foundation for a longer-term research program; the artifact is open at <https://github.com/Ds-GerardoCastro/NEXI>.

**Keywords:** world models, comparative cognition, multi-agent systems, agentic AI, architectural primitives, biological inspiration, animal communication networks, capacity-limited cognition, acerebrate computation, substrate-independent intelligence.
