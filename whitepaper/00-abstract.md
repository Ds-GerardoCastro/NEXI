# Abstract

Modern agentic AI systems built on Large Language Models exhibit a structural failure mode: they cannot reliably plan multi-step behaviour because they lack an internal model of how their actions change the environment. This paper argues that the architectural critique — most clearly articulated by Yann LeCun — has a deeper companion that has not been fully named: AI's biological inspiration is narrow. Modern deep learning descends from a single template, the perceptron, modelled on a simplified mammalian cortical neuron, and propagated unchanged through eighty years of architectural variation. Intelligence as it actually exists in biological species takes many forms; AI has explored only a small slice of that design space.

This paper proposes the **Nature Intelligence Project** as a research program that addresses both critiques jointly. The architectural critique tells us _what is missing_ — internal world models grounded in sensory, not linguistic, prediction. The template critique tells us _where to find candidate designs_ — peer-reviewed comparative cognition across species. The two converge on a single research direction: world models should be informed by the _plurality_ of natural intelligence designs, not by scaling up one cortical template.

The paper introduces **NEXI (Nature Expression of Intelligence)**, the curated catalog that operationalises this direction. NEXI is organised at two layers: individual _patterns_ (architectural primitives) and _clusters_ (coherent intelligence models composed of co-dependent patterns). Each entry is grounded in peer-reviewed natural-system literature, presented in a layered format addressing both engineering and academic audiences, and commits to a falsifiable architectural hypothesis even when no empirical test has yet been run.

A demonstration case study — the **Distributed Social Cognition** cluster derived from research on wild zebra finch communication networks — shows how the methodology produces engineering-ready design primitives with peer-reviewable provenance. The catalog and methodology are presented as the foundation for a longer-term research program; the artifact is open at <https://github.com/Ds-GerardoCastro/NEXI>.

**Keywords:** world models, comparative cognition, multi-agent systems, agentic AI, architectural primitives, biological inspiration, animal communication networks.
