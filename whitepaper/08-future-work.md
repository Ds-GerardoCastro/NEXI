# 8. Future Work

> **Status: stub.** Section to be developed in a subsequent revision.

## 8.1 Catalog growth roadmap

Planned coverage:

- **Near term:** ingestion of additional source papers in non-mammalian, non-primate species, with priority on lineages under-represented in the perceptron-derived design space (cephalopods, eusocial insects, corvids, electric fish, slime molds, plants).
- **Cluster expansion:** as additional sources support existing patterns, drafts promote toward canonical. As patterns from new sources cluster naturally, new clusters emerge.
- **Public release:** the repository becomes public when a critical mass of patterns has been populated and reviewed.

## 8.2 Empirical-validation track

Planned coverage: which NEXIs admit toy implementations soonest, and how empirical evidence — when produced — should be linked back to the catalog. The validation level (literature + falsifiable hypothesis) is intentionally lower than full empirical validation per pattern; this section maps how individual patterns can be promoted through validation in subsequent work.

## 8.3 Community contribution model

Planned coverage: the open contribution gate documented in `docs/methodology.md`. New entries require multi-source evidence and a falsifiable architectural hypothesis. The methodology is intended to be reproducible by other researchers ingesting their own source literature into compatible structures.

## 8.4 Connection to the PhD thesis

Planned coverage: the white paper and catalog are the foundation for a longer-term doctoral research program. Open questions include the venue for empirical evaluation of clusters, the relationship to existing world-model research programs, and whether the Niche-Bound Cognition framing should be developed into its own argument against domain-general AGI as a target.

## 8.5 Open research questions surfaced by the case study

Planned coverage: each cluster surfaces research questions the catalog cannot itself answer. From the Distributed Social Cognition cluster: how to maintain individual identity stability under network reorganisation (MM1 vs. MM4 tension); how to combine structural flexibility with persistent identity (MM5 vs. MM4 tension); whether AGI is a coherent target at all under niche-bound cognition (MM7). These tensions are productive starting points for independent research.
